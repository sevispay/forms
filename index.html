What Do You Know About SEVIS Fee?


What is SEVIS?


SEVIS stands for the Student and Exchange Visitor Information System. It is a web-based system that tracks and monitors international students and exchange visitors in the U.S. International students and exchange visitors must pay this fee to cover the costs of running the student and exchange visitor information system.


How Much is SEVIS Fee for F1 Visa?


All new F-1 students must pay a one time SEVIS fee ($350). F-2 dependents do not have to pay the SEVIS fee.

F-1 students have to pay a SEVIS fee once per program. SEVIS is the Student and Exchange Visitor Information System. The fee funds operation of the U.S. government’s Student and Exchange Visitor Program, which oversees SEVIS.


When to Pay SEVIS Fee?


The SEVIS fee is different from the “MRV fee” (visa processing fee). You do not need to pay the SEVIS fee to make a visa appointment, but the SEVIS fee must be paid at least three days prior to attending your visa interview at the U.S. Embassy or Consulate. Canadian citizens, who are exempt from the visa requirement, must pay the SEVIS fee at least three days prior to requesting for admission to the U.S. at a U.S. port of entry.


Who Needs to Pay SEVIS Fee for J1 Visa?


If you are applying for a J1 Visa to study, conduct research, or participate in an exchange program in the U.S., you must pay SEVIS fee. This applies to both primary and dependent J1 Visa applicants. Failure to pay SEVIS fee can result in delays and rejection of visa application.


How Much is SEVIS Fee for J1 Visa?


Currently, the cost of SEVIS fee for J1 Visa is $220. However, you should check the Official Website of the Department of Homeland Security from time to time for updated costs. Also, keep in mind that SEVIS fee is non-refundable, so it’s best to confirm you are eligible for the J1 Visa before paying.
